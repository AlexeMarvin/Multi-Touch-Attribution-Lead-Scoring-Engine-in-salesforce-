# Multi-Touch Attribution & Lead Scoring Engine (Salesforce Marketing Ops Project)

## The Purpose of This Project

This project demonstrates Marketing Operations and Revenue Operations capability by designing a full multi-touch attribution and lead scoring system inside Salesforce.

The system simulates a real-world marketing-to-revenue engine by combining:
- Campaign structure and UTM-based tracking
- Multi-touch attribution via campaign member relationships
- Automated lead scoring and qualification logic
- MQL lifecycle progression and funnel management
- SDR territory assignment and operational routing
- Executive-level dashboards connecting marketing activity to closed revenue

The goal is to move beyond lead volume reporting and instead connect marketing investment directly to pipeline creation, opportunity conversion, and revenue impact.

---

## What is Contained in the Assets Folder

The `Assets` folder contains all documentation and visualization materials for this project.

Each dashboard is structured as follows:

- A dedicated **Markdown file** describing:
  - Dashboard purpose and business context
  - Funnel logic, attribution model, and KPI definitions
  - Strategic insights derived from marketing, lead, and opportunity data

- A **screenshots folder** containing images of all dashboard visualizations referenced in each Markdown file

Each Markdown file represents a single dashboard and provides a self-contained breakdown of marketing performance, funnel behavior, and revenue impact analysis.

## The TLDR of Each Markdown File

### 1. Executive Revenue Intelligence Dashboard

This dashboard connects marketing activity, attribution performance, segmentation, lead quality, and territory execution into a unified executive view of revenue impact.

#### Top Insights

- **Marketing attribution reveals true revenue-driving channels**
  - Revenue impact varies significantly across campaigns, showing that engagement metrics alone are not reliable indicators of success.

- **Campaign ROI varies widely and should drive budget allocation**
  - A small number of high-performing campaigns generate disproportionate revenue, indicating strong opportunities for budget reallocation.

- **Customer segmentation identifies high-value revenue concentration**
  - Certain industries and ICP segments consistently produce higher revenue outcomes, reinforcing the importance of focused targeting.

- **Lead scoring models require continuous validation against real revenue outcomes**
  - Some high-scoring leads do not convert as expected, while lower-scoring leads may outperform, highlighting the need for iterative scoring refinement.

- **Territory performance impacts overall revenue efficiency**
  - Differences in SDR and regional performance suggest opportunities for improved alignment, balancing, and operational consistency.

---

### 2. Lead Funnel — Qualification & Scoring

This dashboard provides end-to-end visibility into lead progression, scoring accuracy, acquisition performance, and SDR execution across the qualification funnel.

#### Top Insights

- **Lead drop-off occurs at predictable funnel stages**
  - Conversion inefficiencies can be traced to specific lifecycle stages where leads stall or disengage.

- **Lead scoring accuracy is not static and must be continuously tuned**
  - Real-world conversion behavior exposes gaps between theoretical scoring models and actual sales readiness.

- **Lead quality varies significantly by acquisition source**
  - Some channels generate high-volume but low-quality leads, while others consistently deliver stronger conversion potential.

- **Territory and SDR performance is uneven**
  - Differences in MQL generation and conversion efficiency indicate the need for better resource allocation and coaching.

- **Funnel health improves when marketing and sales operate from shared metrics**
  - Unified visibility across teams improves alignment and leads to more consistent qualification outcomes.

---

### 3. Marketing ROI — Campaign Performance

This dashboard evaluates marketing effectiveness by connecting campaign spend, attribution, pipeline influence, and revenue generation.

#### Top Insights

- **Marketing effectiveness must be measured end-to-end, not by top-of-funnel activity**
  - True performance is defined by pipeline and revenue contribution rather than impressions or lead volume.

- **A small subset of campaigns drives most revenue impact**
  - Campaign performance is highly skewed, reinforcing the importance of prioritizing high-performing initiatives.

- **Attribution clarity improves forecasting confidence**
  - Understanding both sourced and influenced pipeline strengthens visibility into marketing’s role in revenue generation.

- **Cost efficiency is a critical driver of marketing ROI**
  - Acquisition costs vary widely, making optimization of spend as important as lead generation itself.

- **Cross-functional alignment improves decision-making**
  - Shared visibility across marketing, sales, and RevOps ensures consistent evaluation of campaign success and investment strategy.
